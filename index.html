About

"Art is not only an act of creation for me; it is a continuous dialogue between practice, research, and critical thought."

Salwa Al Aydi is a compelling figure in contemporary Arab art, where creative practice, academic research, and critical writing converge into a single, dynamic trajectory. She is an Assistant Professor at the Higher Institute of Arts and Crafts of Sfax (ISAMS) and holds a doctorate in the sciences and techniques of art.

Her practice embodies the role of the artist-scholar — one who creates, questions, documents, and advances artistic thought simultaneously.

Her engagement with the plastic arts is both opulent and active, grounded in sustained intellectual commitment and artistic rigor. She has participated extensively in conferences, seminars, and colloquiums at both local and national levels, positioning herself as a forerunner within her field.

Alongside her artistic production, Salwa Al Aydi is a critic and cultural writer. She has published numerous articles in the Tunisian newspaper Essahafa, addressing the evolution of art in Tunisia and offering critical perspectives on contemporary Arab art across regions such as Sharjah, Morocco, and Oman.

Her artistic footprint includes numerous group exhibitions in Tunisia and internationally, including Libya, Morocco, Yemen, Egypt, England, the United Arab Emirates, and Senegal. She organized her first solo exhibition in 2008, followed by a second in 2007, marking key moments in the evolution of her visual language.
